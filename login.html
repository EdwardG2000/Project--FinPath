What is a CCJ and how does it affect my credit score?
A County Court Judgement (CCJ) is a court order issued when you fail to repay a debt. It stays on your credit file for 6 years and can make it very hard to get credit, rent a flat, or sometimes even get a job. The best way to avoid one is to always contact your creditor before missing payments.
How do I remove it?
You can apply to have a CCJ set aside if you have a valid legal reason. If you pay the full amount within one month of the judgement, you can apply to have it marked as "satisfied" which looks better to lenders.
