Lesson 4: APR & Interest

Credit Score Basics

What is APR?

APR stands for Annual Percentage Rate. It's the yearly cost of borrowing money, expressed as a percentage. Unlike a simple interest rate, APR includes fees and other costs associated with a loan or credit card.

💡 Quick tip: A lower APR means you pay less in interest over time. Always compare APRs when choosing between financial products.

How is interest calculated?

Interest is calculated as a percentage of your outstanding balance. With simple interest, you only pay interest on the principal. With compound interest, interest is charged on both the principal and accumulated interest — this can cost you more over time.

For example, a £1,000 loan at 5% APR over one year would cost you £50 in interest — that's £1,050 total repaid.
